The Pirate Game

A fun classroom activity that combines strategy, points, and a bit of competition!

Game Actions

Game Overview

The Pirate Game is an activity where players use a worksheet to place a set of symbols and values on a 7x7 grid. The teacher calls out a grid reference in Spanish, and players must match it to their grid, earning points or using special symbols to interact with the game.

Objective

The goal of the game is to have the highest score by the end. Points can be earned, stolen, swapped, or banked, and special symbols have various effects!

Symbols and Their Effects

The Pirate Ship

This allows the player to 'rob' someone else's points. The player must raise their hand when they get this symbol to choose who to rob.

The Dagger

This allows the player to 'kill' someone and wipe their score down to 0. The player must raise their hand when they get this symbol to choose who to kill.

The Present

This symbol allows the player to give 1000 points to another individual. The player must raise their hand when they get this symbol to choose who to give the points to. A chocolate may also be given to the recipient!

Skull and Crossbones

This symbol allows the player to wipe out an entire row of the class' points to 0. The row can defend if they use 2 shields or mirrors.

Swap Points

This symbol allows the player to swap their points with another person’s points. The player must raise their hand to choose the person to swap with.

Choose

This symbol allows the player to choose the next grid reference to be called out. They must raise their hand to be added to the 'choose next square' list.

Shield

This symbol allows the player to block any wrongdoing against them. The player can use this once the shield is marked in their worksheet.

Mirror

This symbol allows the player to mirror any wrongdoing back to the person who did it. It can be used once and must be marked in the mirror section of their worksheet.

Bomb

If the player gets this symbol, their points will go to 0.

x2

This symbol doubles the player’s current score.

B (Bank)

This symbol allows the player to bank their points, making them safe for the rest of the game. They cannot be stolen or wiped out.

Hitlist

The "hitlist" is used to track players who have the most points for future robs, kills, and point swaps. It's a fun way to add more competition to the game!

Winning the Game

The winner is the player with the most points at the end of the game, including both the banked points and the ongoing score!

Adapted from Mr Collins' Maths Resources on TES. Many versions of 'The Pirate Game' are available online, each offering a slightly different take on the rules.
